01. The Problem Space

In the modern cybersecurity landscape, organizations face an overwhelming deluge of log data. Traditional security monitoring faces three critical failures:

Signal-to-Noise Failure

Security analysts are blinded by volume. Out of 10 million events, only 10 might be malicious. Finding them is the classic "needle in a haystack" problem.

Reactive Blocking

Most firewalls only block known patterns. Sophisticated attackers use unique strategies that bypass static rules, allowing them to hide in plain sight.

Contextual Blindness

Without knowing the IP's origin, the user's history, and the behavioral deviation, a failed login cannot be accurately judged.

The Reality of Analyst Fatigue

SOC analysts spend over 4 hours a day filtering False Positives. SENTINEL is engineered to automate this triage, returning time to the defenders.

04. The 7-Phase Analytical Pipeline

Phase 1: Normalization (parser.py)

Translates messy, unstructured logs (Ubuntu, RedHat, etc.) into a clean, searchable format.

Think of it as a universal translator for computer logs.

Phase 2: Enrichment (enrichment.py)

Adds layers of intelligence: GeoIP (Origin), IP Reputation (History), and MITRE Mapping (Strategy).

Phase 3: Adaptive Calibration (detection.py)

Instead of static rules, SENTINEL learns what "Normal" is for your server using standard deviation math.

05. The AI Analytical Engine

Phase 4 uses Unsupervised Machine Learning to identify deviations from the baseline without needing a blacklist.

Isolation Forest (Anomaly Hunter)

Anomaly Score = 2 ^ (-E[h(x)] / c(n))

Anomalies are "few and different." The model isolates "strange" logins instantly while common logins stay buried deep in the forest.

DBSCAN (Campaign Discovery)

Groups coordinated IP attacks into a single "Campaign Alert," identifying botnets acting in unison.

HMM (User Behavioral States)

Models transitions from [Normal] to [Highly-Risk] behaviors. If a sequence is too improbable for a human, it flags an account takeover.

06. Scoring & Decision Logic

Risk is quantified using a multi-factor weighting model.

Score = (Factor_Fail * 5) + (Factor_AI * 15) + (Factor_Spray * 20)

This ensures that only verified, high-confidence threats trigger a defensive action.

07. Forensic Reconstruction

SENTINEL builds DAG Kill Chains to visualize the attacker's journey.

1. Recon: Finding usernames and service versions.
2. Initial Access: The successful breach event.
3. Persistence: Attempting to stay in the system (Night logins).
4. Privilege Escalation: Trying to gain root/admin control.

08. Tamper Detection (Entropy)

If an attacker deletes logs, it disturbs the natural timeline "jitter." SENTINEL uses Shannon Entropy to detect these mathematical "scars," flagging log tampering even if the evidence was deleted.

09. Active Defense (SOAR)

The SOAR engine (active_defense.py) executes autonomous responses:

  • BLOCK_IP: Kicking the attacker out at the firewall level.
  • LOCK_ACCOUNT: Suspending compromised users instantly.
